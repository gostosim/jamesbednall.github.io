About Me

I am a visiting fellow at the Australian National University, a research affiliate with the ARC Centre of Excellence for the Dynamics of Language (CoEDL) and a linguist at the Anindilyakwa Land Council.

My background and experience spans across the academic and community sectors, involving research, teaching, outreach and community development. My experience in research and the not-for-profit sector includes substantial project management, extensive team work, and international collaboration.

Research

I am interested in language use in its socio-cognitive, cultural and political contexts. My research interests centre around several intersecting domains: i) morphosyntax, semantics and pragmatics, and their interfaces; ii) semantic and structural typology; iii) the relations between language, culture and cognition; and iv) the documentation, description and analysis of Australian Aboriginal languages.

My research builds on over a decade of collaborative work with language communities in western and northern Australia. These strong partnerships underpin my commitment to conducting research that is valuable to the academy and the community alike, and the necessity to support and promote stronger collaborations between (Indigenous and non-Indigenous) linguists and community language researchers, knowledge holders and organisations, in order to achieve this. I work in collaboration with a number of communities in Mid-West Western Australia and north-east Arnhem Land on language revitalisation and maintenance. The languages I have worked most extensively with are Badimaya (Pama-Nyungan, Kartu) from Mid-West WA, and Anindilyakwa (Gunwinyguan) from the Groote Eylandt archipelago, NT.

See Publications and Presentations for a list of and links to my published research.

Language communities I work with

I work and do research with a number of communities in Mid-West Western Australia and north-east Arnhem Land (Northern Territory). In the Mid-West, I have worked with the Malgana, Ngarlawangga, Nhanda, Wajarri, Warriyangka, Yingkarta and Yinhawangka languages and communities, however I have worked most extensively with the Badimaya community, in and around Mt Magnet. In north-east Arnhem Land, the majority of my work has been based on the Groote Eylandt archipelago, working with Warnumamalya on their language Anindilyakwa.
